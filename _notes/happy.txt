Happy Death Day

- almost exclusively white
- only black character who speaks get berated for the crime of eating breakfast at a breakfast meeting.
- reinforces negative stereotypes about women, such as being overly weight conscious and clique culture/passive aggressiveness.
- there is some awareness that these things are bad, but it still feels like the viewer is meant to identify with this kind of culture.
- not much of a murder mystery or a horror movie
- the first and second acts take too long to develop what the audience already knows going into the movie
- the film montages over the murder mystery aspect by quickly discounting all of the people the killer cannot be
- the murderer is quite obvious, as the main character treats her poorly to begin the movie
- the murder mystery aspect does not work because there are no characters who are really developed at all, and would have no motivation for wanting to kill the main character.
- good murder mysteries work when there are many developed characters who have good motivations for wanting to kill somebody, and there are red herrings to throw the viewer off, and clues for the audience the audience to pick up on.
- while there are clues (such as the scrub shoes, the many scenes at the hospital location, being a bad roommate, etc.) it only really points to one person, with no possibility of it being someone else.
- ableist joke (someone pretending to be a deaf person, which is played for laughs).
- no reason for the main character to believe she has infinite lives, or that killing her killer will end the loop.
- no sense of urgency, because she keeps coming back.
- they try to fix this by saying she *could* eventually die, but nothing comes from it.
- no injuries once the loop is closed.
- no explanation for why she is stuck in a time loop, we just know that she is.
- glamorizes sororities/hazing a little bit, even though the main character turns around in the end.
- hard to really identify with the character.
- the third act really picks up the pace, and has some twists that excite the audience.
- the serial killer twist, followed by the cupcake after the "perfect day," and the roommate fight, and the joking gag at the end.
- horror scenes aren't scary, no sustained encounter with the villain, she just dies whenever she meets the roommate.
- too much of the film wasted on playing the same scenes over again.
- the love interest doesn't have much characterization other being the "good guy" who isn't a jerk.
- the romance feels forced, especially since the guy hardly knows the girl because the day keeps resetting.
- the scene where the main character watches another guy watch gay porn was a little awkward, and it was played for laughs. no point to the scene, and the character does not matter to the plot at all.
- he needs a girl to tell him to accept who he is? for all we know, he could be bi, but she dismisses that possibility.
- there was a rape joke, where a guy barges in to a scene where the villain (presumed to be a guy) struggles with the main character on top of a bed, and the guy drunkenly cheers them on.
- it doesn't do any one genre well
- roomate has pretty weak motivation
- repetitve scenes don't add much to the overall plot, other than to say "look! it's a time loop."
- convincing people to believe in the time loop is very tedious, and the love interest just believes her in order to move the plot forward.
- best parts are in the trailer, expecting more.
