Stories about repeating events have existed since Ancient Greece. 

One such story is about Sisyphus, who escapes death by deceiving Zeus and Hades. As punishment, Sisyphus has to push a bolder up a hill, which falls down to the bottom after reaching the summit. He repeats this task, again, and again, for eternity.

<!--more-->

The story of Sisyphus is similar to the story of humans. We repeat a series of routines until we eventually die. Without death, or an endpoint to our routines, we would recognize just how banal our world really is. That is truly terrifying.

"Happy Death Day" tells the story of Tree Gelbman, a college student who continuously relives the night of her murder. When Tree realizes this, she attempts to discover the motive and identity of her masked killer.
